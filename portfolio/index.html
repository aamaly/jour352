Aamaly Hossain is a junior; double majoring in psychology and journalism. Passionate about service and research, she has worked with the International Rescue Committee, helping an Afghan refugee family settle in the U.S., and served on the Student Advisory Board for the Justice and Legal Thought Scholars program.

Hossain has researched the effects of British colonialism on Bangladeshi and Bengali individuals through qualitative interviews and correlational analysis.

On campus, she is active in UNICEF and Phi Alpha Delta and serves as the undergraduate program manager for the Department of Psychology. Hossain is also the co-director of social media for Empowering Women in Law and a writer for Sneaker Theory.

As far as journalism goes, Hossain's love for photography started as just a hobby. She believed this was something she was truly passionate about after taking a co-curriculum journalism class; evetually becoming Eidtor-In-Chief of the Patriot Press, a school publication.

Hossain has a creative eye and loves photographing still images, portraits, events etc., and is always looking for ways to improve her photography knowledge and skills.

After graduating from the University of Maryland, she plans to attend law school and, eventually, pursue a career as a public defender or juvenile attorney.
